# Dan Equity Negotiation — how to honor "50/50" while staying fair to Will + Elena

For the 30-min reactivation call (or the equity follow-up). Dan asked for **50/50**. This is the
playbook to give him a real "50/50" he can point to — on a layer where it's *actually fair* — while
protecting the cash and labor Will + Elena bring.

Created 2026-06-14. Inputs LOCKED: Will ~$50k cash · Elena ~$50k cash · **Dan $0 personal cash**.
Dan's contribution (per Will, 6/14) = **advisory / guidance / network**. He is NOT becoming a CPA and
is NOT the primary capital-raiser. Model: `context/dan-equity-model.md` · calculator: `sourcing/equity_model.py`.

## The principle that makes everything below work

> **50/50 on value you *create together* is fair. 50/50 on value you and Elena *bring in the door* is not.**

Every structure here moves Dan's "50/50" onto the create-together layer (residual upside, the next deal,
an earned ladder) and off the cash-and-labor layer that's purely Will + Elena's.

---

## Structure 1 — 50/50 on the RESIDUAL UPSIDE (the backbone)

The cap table / distributions read close to 50/50 *after* a waterfall pays the right things first:

1. **Return of capital first.** Will + Elena's $100k (plus any outside capital Dan raises) is repaid from
   cash flow **before any profit split**, with a **preferred return (~8%)** on top.
2. **Market salaries to the operators.** Elena (full-time CFO/ops) and Will draw real market pay for the
   work. Dan, hands-off, draws no salary (small board/advisory fee at most).
3. **THEN split the residual 50/50.** Once the cash is returned-with-preferred and the labor is paid, the
   leftover profit + equity value is the pure entrepreneurial residual — and 50/50 on *that* is defensible,
   because the two things Dan doesn't bring (cash, labor) were already made whole off the top.

**Why it works:** Dan hears "50/50" and it's literally true on the upside. Will + Elena are protected —
their money came back with a return and they got paid for the work *before* the even split. This is the
single best answer to "how does 50/50 make sense without actual 50/50."

**The line:** *"Your capital and our salaries come out first — then we're 50/50 on the upside from there."*

---

## Structure 2 — 50/50 on the PLATFORM / Deal #2 (the relationship expander)

Deal #1 is **Will + Elena's** (they fund it, they run it → weighted equity, Dan advisory ~12–15%). Then
Will + Dan go **true 50/50 on the platform / holdco / Deal #2**, where Dan is the one *raising the capital*.

**Why it works:** Dan gets a genuine 50/50 partnership to **build toward** — on the thing he's actually
equipped to co-lead (capital + strategy) — without taking half of the business Will's cash and Elena's
career exit are buying. Often satisfies "I want to be your equal partner" *better* than a slice of Deal #1,
because it's a bigger vision, not a smaller cut.

**The line:** *"Deal one is the one Elena and I are funding and running. The 50/50 I'm excited about with
you is the platform we build on top of it — that's where you raising capital makes us true equals."*

---

## Structure 3 — EARN-UP LADDER to 50% (the lever that removes offense)

Dan **starts at ~15%** (advisory) with a contractual ratchet **up to 50% as he delivers**:

| Dan delivers | Equity moves toward |
|---|---|
| Advisory / guidance / staying engaged (vesting) | ~12–15% (floor) |
| + Raises $X of outside capital | step-ups per $ raised |
| + Sources/closes deals, hits CF milestones | further step-ups |
| + Carries linchpin capital + strategic load | up toward 50% |

**Why it works:** You're not saying no to 50 — you're saying *"50 is on the table, here's the exact path."*
He can't be offended (you offered him 50). The number self-corrects: linchpin contribution earns 50,
advisory-only lands ~15. Aligns the 50 with the contribution that would justify it.

**The line:** *"50's absolutely on the table, Dan — it just comes with carrying the capital raise and
strategic leadership. Here's the ladder; you decide how much of it you want to climb."*

---

## Structure 4 — 50/50 on VOICE, weighted on DOLLARS (if it's really about respect)

If what Dan wants is to be treated as an **equal** — respect, a real say in strategy (his Zone of Genius) —
give him **equal voice on strategic decisions** (board seat, joint sign-off on major moves) while keeping
**economics weighted** to who brought cash + work. People often conflate "I want 50/50" with control, not money.

> ⚠️ **Hard caution:** do NOT give a hands-off partner literal 50% *voting* control over a business Will +
> Elena run daily — that's how a part-time partner deadlocks your operation. Equal voice on *strategy*,
> **Will keeps the operating decision**, and a **buy-sell / tiebreak clause** is non-negotiable.

## Recommended package for the call — LOCKED: #1 (residual waterfall) + #4 (equal voice)

**Will's pick 6/14: Structure 1 and/or 4.** The combination is clean — it gives Dan a *true* 50/50 on the
upside **and** an equal seat at the strategy table, while economics on the value you + Elena bring in the
door stay yours, and operating control stays with the people running it daily. Said as one move:

> *"Here's how I'd want to do this, Dan. Elena and I are putting in the capital and running it day-to-day —
> Elena full-time, me alongside Amazon until I exit in '27. So on the economics: our capital comes back
> first with a preferred return, we draw market salaries for the operating work, and from there the upside
> splits with you as a real partner. And on the big calls — strategy, direction, the moves that matter —
> I want you at the table as an equal; that's the part of this I most want your brain on. The day-to-day
> operating decisions stay with me and Elena since we're the ones in it daily. That's a structure where
> you're a genuine equal where it counts, and one Elena and I can actually commit to."*

**How #1 + #4 split the "50/50" cleanly:**
- **Economics (#1):** 50/50 on the **residual** — *after* (a) Will+Elena's $100k returns with a ~8% preferred
  return, and (b) Elena + Will draw market operator salaries. Dan's headline "equal upside" is real; you're
  protected because cash + labor are made whole first.
- **Voice (#4):** Dan gets **equal say on strategic decisions** (board-level / major moves) — the respect-and-
  equal-partner thing he's likely actually after. **Operating decisions stay with Will + Elena.**

**Non-negotiables to hold:**
1. **Elena is a NAMED co-founder with the LARGEST or co-largest founder stake** (~46% in the model) — she's
   the full-time principal operator and funds half the cash. Never folded into "Will's half." Dead on arrival otherwise.
2. **Dan's equity vests / earns in** — never a flat grant. He's part-time here by his own structure (runs other
   businesses). Vesting protects you if he disengages. Optionally tie step-ups to capital he raises (Structure 3 lever).
3. **Operating control stays with Will + Elena.** Equal voice on *strategy* (#4) is fine; equal *operating*
   control / literal 50% voting is not — a buy-sell / tiebreak clause is non-negotiable.
4. **Return of capital + preferred return + operator salaries come BEFORE any 50/50 split** (#1). This is the
   mechanism that makes Dan's "equal upside" fair to the people who funded and run it.

**The gut-check:** you *can* consciously give Dan more than the ~12% contribution number for the relationship —
the residual-upside structure is exactly that, done in a protected way. Just keep it a **deliberate, defensible
choice**, not a default-accept because "50/50" sounds clean and pushing back feels uncomfortable.
