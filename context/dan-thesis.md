- **Key-man risk** in firms <$500k — one top employee owns most of the client relationships → drives most profit, leaves on transition. Bias toward >$500k revenue + traditional separated org structure.
- **Debt load timing risk** — the May 2025 stall. Mitigated by Scenario B (Elena's cash + time, above). If Elena's contribution changes or shifts, the stall reason returns and the partnership math has to be re-thought.
- **Hard pass sectors**: restaurants, retail, daily-physical-presence requirements. Must be remote-manageable.

## The AZ CPA 51% constraint — two live paths (Dan-licensure ruled out 2026-06-14)

Per AZ law: non-CPAs can hold minority ownership but cannot exceed 49% IF the firm does audits/reviews OR has "CPA" in the name. **Dan is NOT pursuing CPA licensure (confirmed Will, 2026-06-14 — he runs multiple businesses with operators reporting to him; becoming a CPA isn't on the table).** So path 1 is dead; cover comes from one of the two remaining paths, chosen when a concrete deal forces the call:

1. ~~Dan pursues CPA licensure~~ — **ruled out 2026-06-14.**
2. **Retain a CPA as a minority equity holder of the regulated entity** so CPAs hold ≥51% of the *attest* entity. Dan's value here is that his network can *supply* a CPA — that retained CPA is NOT a founder on the partnership cap table.
3. **Avoid CPA-only services** — buy a non-CPA bookkeeping/payroll/accounting firm (non-audit, non-review, no "CPA" in name). Sidesteps the constraint entirely. ~65% of the 505 sourced leads are non-CPA-branded, so this is very workable. Likely the default.

## Tactical doctrine — Mike Warren / Codie / Beshore applied to Dan thesis

Drawn from Mike Warren Business Flipping Course (Will's 4/7 action item, completed ~April 2025) + Codie Sanchez Main Street Millionaire frame + Beshore reference. These are the moves to deploy against actual sellers once the pipeline is live.

**Frames to hold (without arrogance):**
- *Helping frame* — I'm here to solve their problems.
- *Prizing frame* — I'm selective about which deals I pursue.
- *Win-win frame* — real intent to help + mutually beneficial outcomes.

**Off-market sourcing channels:**
- Lawyers and accountants for leads (overlap with Dan's network)
- Direct mail / email — *"would you be interested in selling? If not today, keep me in mind"* — the Apify scrape feeds this
- Search "business for sale by owner with owner financing"
- Identify motivated sellers (retirement, health, fatigue, distress)

**Creative deal structures (when financing is the gating constraint):**
- **Seller-as-bank** — seller takes monthly payments; no SBA needed for that tranche.
- **Selling off the note** — seller sells the note to a third party at a discount for cash-at-close; buyer continues monthly payments.
- **$1M example** — $700k first note (seller sells at close for ~$550–$600k cash) + $300k second note (buyer pays seller monthly).
- **Partner-for-growth pitch** — instead of negotiating price down, offer to bring expertise + resources to grow the business 3×; seller keeps equity + retainer; you take initial equity + performance-based more.

**Initial-conversation rules:**
- Build rapport + understand motivations. NOT a hard sales pitch.
- Preframe credentials (team / advisors, charitable involvement, relevant skills) without arrogance.
- Open-ended questions to reveal motivation — *"What will you do when you retire?" "What's that going to cost monthly?"* — then structure owner financing to match their desired income stream.
- If a seller boasts about untapped potential, ask why they haven't already captured it. Reveals their pain points — top four: **Marketing, People, Systems/automation, Money**.

**Key questions to ask sellers:**
1. Why do you want to sell?
2. Are you willing to provide financing?
3. What's the upside potential?
4. Profitable? Ideally ≥30% net margin?
5. Willing to take a smaller piece of a bigger pie later (retained equity)?
6. Gross sales and net profit?
7. Is the asking price ~3× net profit?
8. Realistic path to triple the profit?

**LOI clauses to write in:**
- **Revenue-floor maintenance** with seller-note adjustment — *firm must maintain X revenue; anything below in next 12 months → $-for-$ reduction in note owed to seller.* Aligns seller's seasonal-handoff incentive with buyer's risk.
- **Seller retention** — previous owner stays on as part-time CFO / consultant for a defined period.

**Industry-specific intelligence (financial services):**
- Renewals typically happen in **January** → time outreach + DD accordingly.
- Sellers willing to talk to **non-CPAs** probably have more favorable org structures for a non-CPA buyer.
- Early access to client business intelligence post-acquisition is a real **side asset** — consulting-for-equity opportunities surface here.
- Lowest-tier financial-advisor license: **Enrolled Agent (EA)** — ~6 months minimum to acquire if Will/Dan need credentials before content creation or fiduciary advice.
