**Anti-goals:**

- Don't let it eat the promo-doc finish, the cut, or relationship keystones.
- Honor the hard filters in `acquisition.md` (customer concentration, owner-dependence, physical-presence, license requirements).

## P — Plan

**Major moves (learn phase):**
1. **Saturday deliberate rep** — 1–2 listings → three-question test → `/deal-eval` → log what you couldn't answer. The gap list is the curriculum. Practice, not shopping.
2. **One broker / known-buyer contact** — sourcing relationships take longest to warm; start now (walking-billboard signal).
3. **Build SBA + SDE literacy** — financing mechanics, reading SDE/add-backs — the diligence muscle you'll need at LOI.
4. **Gmail `Acquisitions` label + weekly scan** via `/acquisition scan-inbox`.

**Realism check:**
- In theory (will this build the muscle?): high — it's straight Codie 3/9/12 learn-phase.
- In practice (follow-through?): **to confirm at quarterly review.** Main risk: acquisition is weekend-weighted (weekdays are Amazon) and the promo window competes. The Saturday keystone is the load-bearing habit.

**Crystal ball — top 3 failure reasons + mitigations:**

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Saturdays slip (busy / promo crunch) — no reps, no filter built | Saturday rep is a dashboard mover + daily-standard weekend keystone, low time cost. Two slipped Saturdays in a row → flag at reflection. |
| Stays purely theoretical — never talk to a broker, no deal flow | One broker / known-buyer contact per month minimum, logged. |
| Jumps to buying too early on excitement | Hard rule: no LOI before ~August. Learn first. |
