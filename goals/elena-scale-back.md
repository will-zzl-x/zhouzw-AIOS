## Die With Zero coast math

### Why not 4% (Trinity Rule)
Trinity Rule preserves principal indefinitely — designed for "leave a pile when you die." Die With Zero (Bill Perkins) explicitly rejects that: optimize for life experience across the curve, end with ~zero.

### Withdrawal rate range for spend-to-zero (~25 retirement years, age 65→90)
| Real return assumption | Spend-to-zero rate |
|---|---|
| 4% real (conservative) | ~6.4% |
| 5% real (middle) | ~5.9% |
| 6% real (optimistic) | ~5.5% |
| 7% real (aggressive) | ~5.1% |

**Using 5.5% as the planning rate** — splits the difference, leaves margin for sequence-of-returns risk, easy to remember.

### Target lifestyle: $120k/yr in today's $
- Portfolio needed at age 65: $120,000 / 0.055 = **$2,182,000 (today's dollars)**
- Years from now to Elena age 65: 34 years (use Elena's age — older partner = shorter horizon)
- 7% real return → $1 today → ~$10 at year 34
- **Coast number today: $2,182,000 / 10 ≈ $218,000 combined invested**

### Where you are
- Will: $94,446 invested
- Elena: TBD (likely $120-200k)
- **Combined: $214-294k estimated → at or past coast**

If Elena ≥ $124k: at coast for $120k/yr lifestyle ✓
If Elena < $124k: small gap to close before coast is real

**Translation:** retirement contributions can drop significantly (or stop) without compromising the $120k/yr retirement lifestyle. The portfolio compounds itself to target.

## Anticipated questions

### "Can her company actually accommodate 20 hr/wk?"
Unknown — Elena needs to ask HR. Some signs to look for:
- Does anyone else in her org work part-time?
- Is the role tied to billable hours / project ownership where 20 hr/wk is awkward?
- Would her manager support it or push back?
- Worst case: company says "no, full-time only" → forces choice between Scenario A (find another part-time gig) or Scenario B (quit).

### "What's her actual 20 hr/wk pay?"
**TBD — she needs to ask.** Some orgs:
- Pro-rate cleanly (50% pay for 50% hours)
- Set a part-time band lower than 50% (e.g., $50k/yr for 20 hr)
- Move to hourly with no benefits (NOT this case — she confirmed full benefits)
- Cap PTO/bonus differently

### "Does scaling now make her job HARDER (same scope, less time)?"
Real risk. Most organizations don't actually scale scope when hours scale. She'd need to negotiate **scope reduction** alongside hours. The ask: "I want to drop to 20 hr/wk and offload [specific responsibilities X, Y, Z] to [person/team]."

### "What about her promotion path / seniority?"
Likely paused at 20 hr/wk. For Scenario A, that may not matter — she's exiting the field anyway in 12-24 months via acquisition. For Scenario B, doesn't matter at all (she's leaving).

### "Can we live on Will's income alone (Scenario B)?"
Math works: $6,300/mo net covers $4k expenses + $350 wedding + leaves ~$1,950 buffer. **But buffer is thin.** Any month with car repair, medical bill, or major spend forces a draw from Elena's $100k taxable. Sustainable on paper, fragile in practice.

### "What if acquisition fails or delays 18+ months?"
Scenario A: ~$45k foregone savings extends pro-rata. Still manageable.
Scenario B: Real strain. Would force Elena back to work or aggressive lifestyle cuts. Mitigation: hard re-evaluation gate at 12 months post-quit.

### "How do retirement contributions change?"
At coast (assumed), aggressive contributions become discretionary. Will continues Amazon 401k match (free money) and Roth IRA backdoor ($7k/yr). Elena contributions stop or reduce. Combined retirement contributions drop ~$15-30k/yr. **Coast covers this.**

### "What about kids?"
Not on the planning table yet but on the horizon (engaged, married Oct 2027). Both scenarios are kid-friendly:
- A: Elena at 20 hr/wk leaves room for child-rearing
- B: Elena fully home for kids
Scenarios actually IMPROVE for kids vs. Scenario C (both full-time + kids = burnout city).

### "What if Will loses his Amazon job?"
Catastrophic in Scenario B (zero income). In A, Elena's $57k + Elena's $100k taxable + $70k S&ME + emergency runway = ~18+ months to find replacement. Either way, mitigation = keep Will's resume + network warm even when not actively job hunting.

### "Healthcare?"
Either scenario: switch to Will's Amazon plan (typically generous coverage). Confirm dental/vision/dependent coverage rolls over. ~$0 additional cost beyond payroll deduction.

### "Tax implications?"
Dropping from $214k → $158k or $101k household:
- A: drops a tax bracket (24% → 22% federal). Net effective rate improves slightly.
- B: drops two brackets (24% → 12-22% federal). Net effective rate ~17%. Real tax savings.
- Both: Roth IRA contribution eligibility may improve (income limits).
- Both: HSA contributions still available.

### "How does scaling now affect the acquisition financing?"
SBA 7(a) underwriting looks at:
- Buyer's down payment (covered: $50k from $70k S&ME)
- Business cash flow (covered: target $213k pre-debt CF)
- Buyer's personal income (Will's $101k continues — primary breadwinner test passes)
- Net worth / liquidity (Elena's $100k + retirement balances are real)
- **Elena's reduced income doesn't materially impact financing.** Will is the primary borrower on personal-guarantee basis.

### "Do we need to tell anyone publicly that Elena is scaling/quitting?"
No. Private decision. The "social signal" version (announcing it on LinkedIn / to broader friend group) can wait until after the wedding or after acquisition close if either of you prefers privacy.

### "What if Elena gets bored at 20 hr/wk or after quitting?"
Real risk. Mitigation: build intentional structure pre-decision, not after. See "What Elena does with her time" under Scenario B. For Scenario A, the natural fill is more accelerator work + wedding planning + dance/Reggie practice + life.
