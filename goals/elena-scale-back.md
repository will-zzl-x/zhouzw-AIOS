## Die With Zero coast math (conservative)

*Framework reference: `references/perkins.md` — Bill Perkins, Die With Zero. Specifically Rule 6 (no autopilot saving), Rule 8 (peak as a date not a number), and the survival threshold formula `0.7 × annual cost × years left`.*

### Withdrawal rate
- Trinity Rule (4%) preserves principal forever → not what you want
- Die With Zero spend-to-zero over ~25 retirement years (65-90)
- **Working assumption: 5.5% real withdrawal rate** (splits 5% conservative / 6% optimistic)

### Real return assumption
- Historical S&P real return: ~7%
- Next-decade consensus (Vanguard/GMO): 4-6% real due to current valuations
- **Working assumption: 6% real return** (conservative middle case)

### Coast number framework
$120k lifestyle / 5.5% = $2.18M target at retirement
÷ 7.25× multiplier (34 years at 6% real, Elena's horizon) = **$300k coast number for $120k/yr**

### "What does 'die with zero' actually mean in practice with no kids?"
You spend the portfolio down over retirement years rather than preserving it. With no inheritance goal, you can target ~$0 at age 90 (or whenever). This means HIGHER lifestyle in retirement OR earlier retirement OR more spending NOW. Bill Perkins' argument is the highest-utility years are 30s-50s, so over-saving is over-paying for low-utility late-life years.
