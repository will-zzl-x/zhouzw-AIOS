## P — Plan

**Major moves:**
1. **Protein ≥ 150g/day** — muscle-preservation lever. Non-negotiable, hit it first. (Running ~169g — over target, fine.)
2. **Calories ~1,820/day** — the deficit, at or just above BMR floor (not below). Track in **Cronometer** every day. No tracking = no deficit = no results.
3. **GVS Ravage 3x/week minimum** — training stimulus preserves muscle in a deficit. Hold loads, don't chase PRs.
4. **10,000 steps/day** — passive expenditure. Add steps before cutting calories further if the scale stalls.
5. **Morning weigh-in daily** — feedback loop. Read the **7-day rolling average**, never spot weights.

**Realism check:**
- In theory (will this plan work?): 90% — ~1,820 cal + training + steps produces a deficit. It's math.
- In practice (will I follow through?): 80% — week 1 executed clean (−3.0 lb, protein over, steps in band). Main risk is Cronometer adherence on weekends/social dinners.

**Action on adherence:** Log food in Cronometer before eating when possible (front-load the decision).

**Crystal ball — top 3 failure reasons + mitigations:**

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Calories creep on weekends / social dinners — deficit disappears | Same 1,820 target all 7 days; log everything in Cronometer. Let the rolling avg steer, not single days. |
| Protein falls short → deficit eats muscle | Protein first at every meal; hit 150g before filling remaining calories. |
| Training intensity craters under the deficit | **Abort canary: Larsen Press drops >10% → bump to ~2,000 cal.** Sessions stay in the calendar, same slots. |

## S — System

**Tracking:**
- Cronometer: daily calories + protein
- Morning scale: daily weigh-in, 7-day rolling avg, log via `/health-os`
- Weekly: `/health-os check` every Sunday — avg calories, protein hit rate, weight trend
- Training: `/workout-log` every session — flag if strength drops (early muscle-loss signal)

**Refeed trigger:** 7-day rolling avg ≤176 lb → add one refeed day/week (maintenance cals, fat ≤40g, carbs 350g+). Don't react to single weigh-ins.

**Reminders:** Nutrition + steps are daily targets on every check; morning coffee surfaces them; the scale is the daily feedback loop.

**Accountability:** Weight trend visible weekly. If the rolling avg isn't moving by ~June 7 (3 weeks in), audit the Cronometer log for calorie creep before adjusting targets.
