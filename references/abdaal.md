---

## Core Principle

Yearly goals fail because they're too distant and too many. 90-day quarterly quests work because the timeframe is concrete, the scope is limited, and you can see the finish line.

**The constraint that makes it work:** 1 Work Main Quest + 1 Life Main Quest per quarter. Everything else is a Side Quest — bonus, not required.

---

## Quest Structure

### Main Quest (1 Work + 1 Life)
- Must achieve by end of 90 days — non-negotiable
- Should be the single most important thing in each area
- Verifiable success criteria only — not "get healthier," but "172 lb by Jul 4"

**5 elements of a well-formed quest:**
1. **Name** — short, memorable
2. **Why** — why is this the most important thing right now
3. **Win condition** — hard, verifiable criteria for success
4. **Excitement** — why this quest genuinely excites you
5. **Milestones** — what must be done by when to stay on track

### Side Quests (1–3 per area)
- Bonus, not required
- Drop or deprioritize if Main Quest is at risk
- Don't get equal daily attention

---

## Daily System

**3 required daily targets** — non-negotiable minimum for the day:
1. One tied to Work Main Quest
2. One tied to Life Main Quest (usually fitness/health)
3. One wildcard (relationship, side quest, admin)

**Done threshold:** 2 of 3 = acceptable day. All 3 = complete day. 0 = missed — don't make it 2 in a row.

**1 priority per day** — not a to-do list. One thing that, if done, makes the day a success regardless of everything else.

**3–5 major moves per quest** — the key actions that would actually move the quest forward if done consistently. Not a task list — a leverage list.

---

## Weekly Review

Revisit quests every week:
- Am I on track for the win condition?
- What's the single most important thing this week for each quest?
- What got in the way last week and how do I remove it?

Weekly review is what separates people who set quests from people who complete them.

---

## GPS Method (Goal → Plan → System)

For any quest that's stalling:
1. **Goal** — is the win condition clear and verifiable? If not, clarify first.
2. **Plan** — do you have a concrete sequence of actions that would achieve the goal if executed?
3. **System** — do you have a recurring trigger (time, place, habit stack) that ensures the plan actually runs?

Most stalling happens at System, not Goal. The goal is clear, the plan exists, but there's no reliable trigger to execute it.

---

## Quarterly Rhythm

- **Week 1:** Set quests, define win conditions, map milestones
- **Weeks 2–11:** Execute, weekly review each Sunday
- **Week 12:** Quarterly reflection — what moved, what didn't, what carries forward

Quarterly reflection feeds directly into next quarter's quest selection.

---

## How Many Quests Is Too Many

**Hard limit:** 1 Main Work Quest + 1 Main Life Quest. Period.

Side quests can stack (1–3), but if you're running 5 main quests simultaneously you're running 0 — attention dilutes to nothing. The constraint is the point.

**The trap:** labeling everything "main quest" because it feels important. Only one thing is the most important thing.

## Application to Will's AIOS

Will's Q2 setup has 5 named quests across 5 areas. Under Abdaal's framework:

| Quest | Classification |
|-------|---------------|
| Career — "Build the Promo Doc" | **Work Main Quest** |
| Fitness — "Execute the NYC Cut" | **Life Main Quest** |
| Relationships — "Court Elena" | Side Quest |
| Money — "Find the Business" | Side Quest |
| Wedding — "Clear the Stack" | Side Quest |

**Key implication:** Career and Fitness get equal daily presence (they're both Main Quests). Relationships, Money, and Wedding move when there's bandwidth — they don't compete with the main two on daily priority.

**Daily targets for Will (Abdaal-aligned):**
1. **Career** — 1 SCM II behavior logged or promo doc advanced
2. **Fitness** — Ravage session (training day) OR 10k steps + 150g protein (off day)
3. **Side** — one move from relationships, money, or wedding (rotate by urgency)

This matches what `context/daily-standard.md` already implements.
