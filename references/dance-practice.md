# Bachata Practice Framework

Will's self-directed practice system for bachata sensual — the daily-habit layer that sits *under* paid privates and makes them worth the money. Established 2026-07-13.

**Why this exists:** Privates are a recurring discretionary spend Will wants ROI on. A private teaches; only daily reps *install*. Without a home-practice habit, the money buys instruction that decays before the next lesson. This framework converts privates into compounding skill.

**System linkage:** Dance is a **standing desire-polarity move** (`goals/desire-polarity.md` — Perel interior-life + a distinctive skill Elena isn't the source of) AND a Life-Main "point of origin" vector (expressive play built for its own sake, not for her response) AND a future-location criterion (annual review: bachata scene is quest-infrastructure). Practicing alone, for the joy of it, unnarrated, *is* the differentiation rep — it's not separate from the quest, it's a direct expression of it.

## The Four Pillars (rotation — one focus per day, ~1–2 songs is enough)

The whole regimen fits in the time it takes to play one or two tracks. Volume is not the goal — daily contact is. One or two songs a day beats an hour once a week.

### 1. FUNDAMENTALS — body movement
Isolations · hip movement · upper-body movement · weight transfers. Go back to the base whenever technique feels flashy-but-hollow. This is the pillar everything else rides on.

### 2. FOOTWORK — depth over flash
"It doesn't need to be flashy." **Protocol:** pick ONE four-count, drill it **both sides of the body for a full week** at home, *then* sprinkle it into social dancing. One clean pattern, both sides, socially deployed = more than enough. Do not collect patterns; install one at a time.

### 3. MUSIC — passive + active listening
Listen while driving, laundry, working out — bachata is the default audio. **Active drills inside the passive time:** find the counts mid-song · spot the breaks · pick out one specific instrument and track it. This pillar costs zero dedicated time; it rides on time already spent.

### 4. FREEDOM TO PLAY — the expressive muscle
"If you're not making time to be expressive in your own training, why expect your body to dance playfully with a partner?" **One or two songs a day of freestyle bachata** — alone, no mirror-grading, no outcome. This is the pillar most likely to get skipped and the one that most directly feeds partner connection. It is also the purest desire-polarity rep in the whole system: expressive play, for itself, un-scored.

## Private-lesson homework
Whatever the current private assigns is the **priority overlay** on top of the rotation — homework first, then the day's pillar. Capture each lesson's homework via `/mid-week-capture` so it doesn't evaporate before the next session; log it here or in the capture inbox.

**Current homework (private ~2026-07-13):** body-rolls + transitions sequence. Overlay these on the daily rep until the next private; most map to FUNDAMENTALS (isolations/body movement).

*Transitions & posture:*
- **Keep the plank, keep your line on the transition** — turn *in place*, not a weird directional turn.
- **No slouch on the transition** (recurring correction) — stay tall through it.
- Stepping variation: **5, 6 → L, and L — the second L stops.** Step toward partner.
- Trace/stop → **transition to shadow.**

*Shadow position:*
- Step up, **rock forward on the right.** Posture check: **nose over the back foot, back foot behind you.** (Don't forget this one.)

*Body-roll sequence (the core homework):*
- **Chest roll — TUCK FIRST.** The tuck creates the connection and must happen *before* the roll, not during. Finish the chest roll → into a **hip roll.**
- **Variation 1:** hip roll → **snake** (go forward, set the frame a little before moving).
- **Variation 2:** chest roll → **up → concave forward → head roll →** finish with the **frame going forward** (should *feel* like you're traveling forward).
- Goal: chain all of it into one smooth pass (chest → hip → snake / concave → head), not four separate moves.

*This week's FOOTWORK focus:* the **5,6 L-L (second L stops)** step — both sides, all week, then sprinkle into socials.

## The daily habit (what actually gets programmed)

- **Micro-rep, daily:** 1–2 songs. Homework overlay + that day's rotating pillar. Surfaced as a Daily Consistent.
- **Passive music:** running by default (car/chores/lifting) — no scheduling needed.
- **Weekly footwork focus:** one four-count, both sides, all week, then social-deploy.
- **Anti-goal:** don't let it become a graded performance. Freedom-to-play especially is ruined by scoring — same rule as the Joy anchor.

**Tracking:** binary daily (did the micro-rep happen), noticed lightly in the weekly reflection's relationship/Life-Main section. Never grade the *quality* of the dancing — that rebuilds the scoreboard the whole point-of-origin quest is dismantling. Grade only that the rep happened.
