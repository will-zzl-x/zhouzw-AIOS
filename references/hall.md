# Steve Hall (Revive Stronger) — Cutting Framework

Secondary fitness reference, supplementary to Schofield (`references/schofield.md`,
the primary authority). Use Hall to sharpen cutting mechanics where Schofield is
directionally right but coarser-grained. Flag any real contradiction against
Schofield before applying — none found on first pass (2026-07-10 ingest).

Source: Revive Stronger podcast/YouTube content on cutting, fat loss, and diet
adherence.

---

## Deficit Sizing (sharper version of Schofield's flat 500–800 cal rule)

Scale deficit to current leanness, as %bodyweight/week loss:

- **Under 10% BF:** 0.25–0.5%/week
- **10–15% BF:** 0.5–1%/week
- **15–20% BF:** 1–1.5%/week
- **Above 20% BF:** 1.5–2%/week

Use this as the precision tool going forward instead of (or alongside) Schofield's
flat calorie-deficit range — same "faster when fatter, slower when leaner"
principle, just calibrated to bodyweight rather than a fixed calorie band.

## Protein

~1g/lb, up to 1.2g/lb when leaner or cutting aggressively. Diminishing returns
past that. Matches Schofield's ~150g/day target almost exactly — no change to
practice.

## Training on a Cut

Training is a muscle-retention tool, never a calorie-burning tool. Same
principle as Schofield, stated near-verbatim.

## Steps / Cardio

- Floor: 7–8k steps/day. Cap: ~15k.
- Max increase over habitual level: ~25%.
- Prefer LISS over HIIT.

Matches Schofield's "10–12k optimal, 15k+ diminishing returns" — same
directional guidance, tighter band.

## Refeeds / Diet Breaks

Mostly psychological/adherence tools, not physiologically required — more
useful the leaner you are and the longer you've been dieting. Not needed early
in a cut or above ~15–20% BF. Matches Schofield exactly.

## Sleep as a Fat-Loss Lever

Losing just 1 hr/week of sleep measurably reduces fat-loss rate (cited study).
Separate 2017 meta-analysis: each hour under 7 hrs/night raises obesity risk
~9%. Reinforces Schofield's sleep/cortisol/P-ratio section — sleep is a fat-loss
lever, not just a recovery tool.

## New Concepts (not in Schofield — worth using)

**Satiety-to-calorie ratio.** High water + fiber content wins on satiety per
calorie. Concrete example: a boiled potato is ~7x more satiating than a
croissant, gram for gram. Useful lens for food choice beyond just "energy
density," which Schofield already covers — this adds the fiber/water framing
specifically.

**Thermic effect of feeding, whole vs. processed.** Cited study: a whole-food
sandwich burned ~140 cal in digestion vs. ~75 cal for a processed sandwich at
matched macros. Concrete, citable number behind "80% wholesome / 20% flexible"
— not just a vibe.

**High energy flux.** At the same net deficit, eating more *and* moving more
tends to outperform eating less and moving less. If progress stalls, the lever
to pull may be "get more active" rather than "cut harder."

**Diet duration rule of thumb.** Losing 10%+ of bodyweight drops TDEE more than
body-comp changes alone would predict. Practical target: 5–10% total bodyweight
loss per cutting phase before a break/re-baseline. Close to the abort-signal
logic already in `state.md` (re-baseline around ~177).

**Planning tomorrow's eating the night before** is, per Hall, the single
highest-leverage adherence tool. Validates the existing Cronometer-logging
habit rather than adding new practice.

## Minor / Noted, Not Actionable

- Chrono-nutrition (front-loading calories earlier in the day): Hall himself
  says the effect is small, mostly irrelevant once already in a deficit.
- Weighted vests: Hall ran a full contest prep without one and did *better* —
  calls the evidence weak. No action needed.

## Meal-Prep / `meal-planning/` Relevance

- **Eat out less, home-cooked = better results** (estimation error compounds
  with restaurant food) — direct validation of why the cook-cycle system
  exists.
- **80/20 flexible dieting, not strict "clean"** — supports keeping genuinely
  indulgent recipes in rotation rather than making every recipe austere.
- **Larger, less-frequent satisfying meals beat small frequent snacking** —
  reinforces the existing M1–M4 meal-slot structure over grazing.
- **Concrete micronutrient target:** 3–4 combined fruit/veg servings per 1,000
  cal, 14g fiber per 1,000 cal. Candidate checkable target for recipe vetting
  (`meal-planning/ai/vet_recipe.md`) alongside macros.
