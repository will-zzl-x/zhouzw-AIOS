## Stub

Schnarch's framework centers on differentiation — the ability to maintain your own identity, values, and selfhood within intimacy rather than merging or accommodating to reduce anxiety. The "sexual crucible" is the idea that intimate relationships create pressure that can either destroy you or forge growth.

Relevant to Will's context: the desire dynamic with Elena. Schnarch and Perel are aligned — both argue that desire requires two separate people, not merger. Over-accommodation and over-pursuit kill desire.

Key Schnarch principle: the partner who wants more connection is often the one who needs to differentiate more, not pursue harder.
