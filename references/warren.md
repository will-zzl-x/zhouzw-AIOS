# Mike Warren — Business Flipping Course Reference

Source: Will's notes from Modules 1-2, captured ~April 2025. ~13 months old as of 2026-05-31.

## Three Frames

Three buyer postures Warren teaches to flip the seller power dynamic. Implement without arrogance.

- **Helping frame** — You are not asking to buy. You are showing up to solve a problem the owner already has (succession, burnout, can't grow it, can't exit). The buyer is the answer, not the asker.
- **Prizing frame** — You are selective. You evaluate them as much as they evaluate you. Walk-away energy. Talk about your other deals in the pipeline. The seller should feel they are competing for you.
- **Win-Win frame** — Every structure benefits both sides; never extract. Frame deferred payments / earnouts / equity rolls as upside for the seller, not concessions you are squeezing.

## Mindset

- Continuous learning + positive thinking ("legs to support a belief").
- Branding & Positioning — "customers buy time with family, not tax prep." Have a name for what you do (LLC, website, one-line bio) so you look like a real acquirer rather than a tire-kicker. Most casual buyers don't even have a website — the bar is low.
- Unique proprietary model — name your buy box narrowly.
- Celebrity authority (later-stage) — borrowed credibility via testimonials, podcast appearances, association membership, advisor stack.
- Social proof — case studies of prior deals, or mentors / partners with track records if you're a first-timer.
- Scarcity + selectivity — "I only do 1-2 deals per year." Combined with the Prizing frame, flips perceived urgency.

Underlying logic: a seller's biggest fear is selling to someone who breaks the business or stiffs the note. Every pitch element exists to retire that fear before money is discussed.

## Dealing with Brokers

Warren's rule: brokers are gatekeepers, not allies — their commission incentive is misaligned with yours.

- Always try to reach the seller directly. Brokers filter and spin.
- Prefer unsophisticated or hard-to-reach brokers (regional, single-shingle, older) over polished M&A advisors. Less competition on their listings, less price-anchoring, more willingness to accept creative structure.
- Never reveal your full buy box to a broker — they will price up to your ceiling. Disclose only the parameters needed to qualify the deal in front of you.
- If the broker won't let you talk to the seller before LOI, that's a yellow flag — either the seller is being managed, the financials don't hold up to direct questions, or both.
- Owner-financing receptivity is the single most important screen.

## Off-Market Sourcing

Warren leans heavier on direct off-market than Codie does.

- Lawyers and accountants for leads. The 4A network — accountants, attorneys, agents (real estate / insurance), advisors (financial). Offer a sourcing fee ($5k-$15k per closed deal) to get on their list.
- Direct mail to owners in target SIC/NAICS codes pulled from D&B or county business license lists.
- LinkedIn outreach to owners 55+ in the target sector with personalized notes, not pitch decks.
- Direct mail/email campaigns; search "business for sale by owner with owner financing."
- Industry associations + chambers of commerce — show up in person.
- Walk-in / drive-by / cold call for local service businesses and trades where the owner answers the phone.
- 7-D motivated-seller targeting (cross-ref Sanchez framework).

Will's Apr 22, 2025 outreach run scraped 131 leads on a free tier — Warren-style off-market sweep. Outreach script drafted in same session (held in separate tab).

## Owner Financing & Creative Structure

Warren's distinctive move: treat the seller note as a tradeable instrument, not just a payment plan.

- **OPM (other people's money) preferred** — bank debt, seller carry, third-party note buyers, investor partners.
- **Standard seller carry** — seller takes 10-30% as a note. Buyer pays SBA + down + note.
- **Standby seller note** — payments deferred 24-36 months. SBA SOP 50 10 allows the standby note to count toward equity injection. This is the lever that unlocks the $700k-$1M stretch range in the deal box.
- **Earnout / revenue-protection clause** — if revenue drops below threshold X in 12-24 months post-close, note principal reduces dollar-for-dollar. Protects buyer from misrepresented financials and signals to seller that buyer trusts the business but is hedging the handoff.
- **Sell the seller note at closing** — the signature Warren example. Structure: $1M business = $700k SBA 1st mortgage + $300k seller note. Seller sells the $700k note at closing for $550-600k cash to a third-party note buyer. Seller walks with near-cash at close + collects $300k debt service over time. Buyer keeps the original note terms, paying the new note holder. The spread is the deal — buyer effectively gets seller financing without the seller having to wait.
- **Growth-partnership-into-acquisition** — start with a sweat-equity arrangement (cut of incremental revenue you generate, similar to Codie's Venmo Challenge). Prove fit + numbers for 6-12 months. Convert equity stake + seller note into full ownership at a pre-negotiated multiple. Lower-risk on-ramp when the seller is hesitant to commit to a sale.

## The Pitch

- **Initial conversation = rapport + motivation discovery, NOT hard sell.**
- **Preframing** — control the narrative before the meeting. Send a one-pager or short video describing who you are, what you buy, what you've done. Seller walks in already conditioned to view you as the buyer (not a buyer). Mention achievements, charity involvement, growth/relationships skills. Position as having knowledge/experience even if it's borrowed via team/advisors.
- **Partner-for-growth offer** — "I'll help triple value. You stay involved with a management fee + retained equity. I get equity at start + more on performance."
- **Open-ended motivation questions** — "What will you do after the sale?" → "What will that lifestyle cost you per month?" → tailors the owner-financing structure. If they need $8k/mo to live, a $1M lump sum and a $50k down + $8k/mo seller note buy the same outcome — but the note is far cheaper for you and tax-friendlier for them.
- **Pain-point reveal** — "If the business has so much potential, why haven't you achieved it?" Exposes Marketing / People / Systems / Money pain.
- **Position as selective** — imply you are evaluating fit. Combined with Prizing frame.

## Key Seller Questions

Verbatim list from extraction:

- Why do you want to sell? (motivation — surfaces the 7Ds and what the deal must protect)
- What will you do after the sale? What will that lifestyle cost you per month? (structures the owner-financing conversation)
- What's the potential of this business — and why haven't you achieved it? (reveals pain points and what to underwrite — Marketing / People / Systems / Money)
- Would you consider seller financing? (always ask — even a no opens the door to creative carry structures)
- Is the business profitable? Gross / net?
- Is your asking price roughly 3x net profit? (anchors in industry-standard math without you naming a number first)
- Would you take a smaller piece of a bigger pie? (sets up retained equity / partnership structures)
- What's the potential to triple the business?
- What matters most to you in a sale — price, legacy, employees, customers, speed of close? (ranks priorities so you know which lever to pull)
