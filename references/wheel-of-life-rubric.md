# Wheel of Life — Objective Rating Rubric

Instrument for `/quarterly-review` and `/annual-review`. Built 2026-07-12 (first annual bootstrap) so scores are reproducible and comparable across reviews instead of vibes. Rate every axis against the bands below; scores from different quarters are only comparable if they used this rubric.

**How to rate:** find the highest band where you meet all (or clearly most) markers — that's your band. Pick the low or high number within it by instinct. Markers are behaviors and countable facts, never feelings — per the house doctrine: measure inputs, don't score outputs.

**Universal bands (same meaning on every axis):**

| Band | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 9–10 | Compounding | The system runs itself and produces growth without strain |
| 7–8 | Working | Consistent behavior, targets mostly hit, no active drag |
| 5–6 | Maintaining | Stable but passive — nothing deteriorating, nothing deliberately advancing |
| 3–4 | Drifting | A known gap with no working system against it; costs showing up |
| 0–2 | Broken | Active damage, avoidance, or crisis |

---

## Mission — is the work aligned with the exit thesis?

Rated on **allocation and movement**, not day-job mood. Instruments: `backlog.md` quest rows, `journals/acquisition-log.md`, obligation ship-dates.

- **9–10:** Quest win-conditions on pace or ahead; day job at true maintenance (obligations ship on time, zero guilt-carry); weekly time visibly flowing to the exit vehicle.
- **7–8:** Main quest moves every week (reps logged, contacts made); obligations ship at most one week late.
- **5–6:** Quest exists and is believed in, but movement is sporadic — keystone reps firing <50% of their cadence.
- **3–4:** Main quest stalled ≥3 consecutive weeks; obligations carried past multiple self-set deadlines.
- **0–2:** No quest, or daily dread of the work.

## Money — is the machine running?

Instruments: `context/financial-state.md` allocations, savings rate, NW trajectory.

- **9–10:** All autos fired ($2k acquisition + Roth + wedding); savings rate ≥30%; NW up on the quarter; zero unplanned spends >$500.
- **7–8:** All autos fired; savings 25–30%; buffer spends deliberate (routed by the standing rule, not impulse).
- **5–6:** Autos fired but leakage — untracked spending, rate <25%, or buffer routinely evaporates with no memory of where.
- **3–4:** Missed an auto-allocation, or dipped into an earmarked fund off-plan.
- **0–2:** New debt, or the acquisition fund raided.

## Growth — applying or consuming?

Rated on **artifacts produced**, not content consumed. Instruments: shipped systems/docs/evals, demonstrable new capability.

- **9–10:** Producing weekly (deal evals, shipped tools, written docs) AND one new capability proven in use this quarter.
- **7–8:** Apply-to-consume ratio clearly favors applying; ≥1 real artifact most weeks.
- **5–6:** Learning is happening but it's mostly intake — courses, videos, books without output.
- **3–4:** Comfort-zone reps only; the skill named as "the edge" plateaued another quarter.
- **0–2:** Neither learning nor producing.

## Body — is the physical system executing its phase?

Instruments: `journals/workout-log.md`, step counts, 7-day weight avg vs. phase band, lifting canary.

- **9–10:** 5–6 sessions/wk; steps ≥12.5k on ≥80% of days; weight inside the phase band; canary green; visible composition progress.
- **7–8:** ≥3 sessions/wk (the floor); steps ≥12.5k on ≥70% of days; weight within ~1 lb of phase band; canary green/yellow-explained.
- **5–6:** Sessions at or just below floor; steps hitting 50–70%; weight drifting but <2 lb off band.
- **3–4:** <3 sessions/wk for 2+ weeks, or weight >2 lb off band for 2+ weeks with no adjustment made.
- **0–2:** Not training.

## Mind — clarity, stress, cognitive energy

Instruments: digital-minimalism gates (Tier status, no reinstalls), deep-work blocks, sleep (proxy until tracked: phone-off-nightstand adherence), derailed-day count.

- **9–10:** Sleep tracked and ≥7.5h avg; daily deep-work block; zero doomscroll relapses; ≤1 day/quarter lost to rumination.
- **7–8:** Phone rules holding (nightstand + Shorts-dead + no reinstalls); ≥3 deep blocks/wk; ≤1 derailed day per week.
- **5–6:** Rules mostly holding but untracked; clarity fine, no deliberate practice sustaining it.
- **3–4:** A relapse (reinstall, Shorts back, phone in bed) or chaotic sleep for 2+ weeks.
- **0–2:** Fried — can't hold attention, stress running the day.

## Heart — reactivity and self-soothing

The May 2026 baseline marker: "a rejection can cost a full day." Instruments: recovery half-life of a hit, `/mid-week-capture` self-soothe routing, D.E.E.R. catches.

- **9–10:** Hits metabolized within the hour; self-soothe routing automatic; friction doesn't change the day's plan.
- **7–8:** Recovery same-evening; ≥1 logged catch-and-redirect per week; venting → capture routing is the norm.
- **5–6:** A hit costs an evening; routing happens sometimes; no day-plus spirals this quarter.
- **3–4:** Hits still cost a full day+ (May baseline); venting/grading beats routing.
- **0–2:** Mood is hostage to outcomes daily.

## Romantic — YOUR inputs, never her response

Hard constraint from `goals/desire-polarity.md`: grade Will's behaviors only — never encounters, never Elena. The north-star signal (desire-fuel vs. love-fuel) is *observed* in the review's notes, not scored.

- **9–10:** Input behaviors ≥80% (solo-space reps, presence beats, routine-variation); conflicts repaired same-day; zero scoreboard leaks into speech; night-talk rule intact all quarter.
- **7–8:** Inputs firing most weeks; rules intact; repairs land within a day.
- **5–6:** Inputs sporadic; no active damage; connection running on default-availability rather than choice.
- **3–4:** Scoreboard leaking (grading out loud, reaction-watching); repairs delayed or re-litigated; over-pursuit uncaught.
- **0–2:** Active disconnection or the dread-line crossed.

## Family — touchpoint cadence

No target existed before this rubric; setting one: **≥2 meaningful contacts/month** (mom, brother — calls count, texts don't).

- **9–10:** Weekly-ish contact plus a visit planned or executed this quarter.
- **7–8:** ≥2 real contacts/month, at least some initiated by Will.
- **5–6:** ~1/month, mostly reactive.
- **3–4:** Month-plus gaps; contact only when prompted.
- **0–2:** Estrangement or unresolved conflict left cold.

## Friends — the 2-week cadence

Instrument: the standing touchpoint target + depth events.

- **9–10:** Cadence hit ≥90% of windows; ≥1 depth event (trip, long dinner, real one-on-one) this quarter; Will initiating.
- **7–8:** Cadence ≥75% of windows; initiated at least half.
- **5–6:** Cadence ~50%, or one big deposit (a trip) covering weeks of silence around it.
- **3–4:** Cadence dead ≥4 weeks with no repair attempt.
- **0–2:** Isolated.

## Joy — do wins land? (retrospective recall only)

**Compatibility note:** the daily Joy anchor is explicitly never tracked or graded (`goals/desire-polarity.md`) — this axis is rated by *retrospective recall at review time*, which is allowed. Markers: (a) the last-win test — name the most recent real win; did satisfaction persist ≥ a day, or did "next" eat it immediately? (b) intrinsic time — blocks spent on things with no instrumental payoff (violin, dance, play, reading for pleasure); (c) the "not enough" hum — how many days/week did it show up?

- **9–10:** Wins land and stay landed; ≥3 intrinsic-time blocks/wk; hum rare (few times a month).
- **7–8:** Wins mostly land; hum ≤1–2 days/wk; regular intrinsic time.
- **5–6:** Mixed — wins land then evaporate within a day; hum most weeks; intrinsic time squeezed by optimization.
- **3–4:** Winning on paper, not feeling it (May 2026 baseline); hum most days; every activity instrumentalized.
- **0–2:** Anhedonic grind.

---

**Maintenance:** if a marker becomes obsolete (phase change, new quest), update the marker — never rate against a dead marker. Rubric changes get a dated note here so cross-quarter comparability is auditable.
